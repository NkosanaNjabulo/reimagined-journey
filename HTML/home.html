How Instant Messaging impacts our personal lives

Introduction

What is Instant Messaging?

Instant Messaging is a form of technology that allows for communication over long distances. Instant Messaging was invented in 1971, and was designed to be used on desktop computers. It is however with the invention of cell phone and their common adoption that instant messaging became the worldwide sensation we know today. Instant Messaging has come a long way since its invention and has taken many different forms since then. Today IM platforms include photo and video sharing, voice memos and live-location sharing. Platforms such as WhatsApp even allow for phone call and video calls over the internet and are still developing newer features even today.


How has Instant Messaging changed the way we communicate?

Although Instant Messaging came into prominence after the invention of telephonic communication, it still had a significant impact on the way we interact and stay in touch with each other as a society. Instant Messaging made communicating more practical and less time consuming than phones at the time. It also came with the further advantage of being much cheaper and had constant rates whether you were texting someone right next to you or halfway across the world. This promoted constant use of the service and relegated phone calls for more consequential situations.
