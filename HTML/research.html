Effects of Instant Messaging

Introduction

Effects on Mental Health

According to the research I conducted, Instant Messaging has proved do have minimal effect on our mental health state and psyche. The adoption of instant messaging into human life was no smooth transition but we have gotten to a point where the technology has been integrated seamlessly into the human social patterns.

Effects on Work-Life Balance

Balancing work and home life is a skill that requires discipline and the ability to separate the two. With that being said Instant Messaging has proved to have a detrimental effect on work-life balance. Being able to be accessible to your work at home and your home at work muddles the boundaries between the two. This results in poorer balance between the two and ultimately unhealthy habits. The effects of this can be countered by separating the IM services used for work and personal life or furthermore, using separate devices for the two.

Effects on Sociability

Our sociability has been hugely benefited from Instant Messaging. The service has allowed us to not only connect with those we know but also form new connections and relationships. It has also allowed us to communicate cheaply which further encourages more of this type of communication.
Overall Instant Messaging has allowed us to stay safe by being able to check-in on our loved one,
form new and interesting relationships and participate in our communities and social circles' group
chat.
